**Subject:** Craftsmanship essay, from the SonicGen side

Nathan,

I read your craftsmanship piece a few months ago and the line about gnarly codebases hindering AI tools is the clearest version of something I keep running into from the other direction. I spent Sep through Dec 2025 writing an audio fingerprinting engine by hand in Python, no agents, around 14,000 lines, because I wanted to see what I still knew without them. The thing I got wrong wasn't code. I picked Supabase for hash storage when a local SQLite file was the obvious answer, and no amount of agent help would have caught that. It was a judgment call about what belongs where, and I wrote it up honestly: https://github.com/MLGalusha/SonicGen/blob/main/DEEP_DIVE.md. Is there 15 min next week to talk about how the AI Foundations team thinks about that boundary between judgment and generation?
