# Cover Letter -- Cursor, Forward Deployed Engineer

I use Cursor every day. It is my primary editor and the tool I build production systems with. When I apply for this role, I am not pitching a hypothetical interest in the product. I have logged hundreds of hours directing agents through it, and I have opinions about where it works well and where it does not. That kind of product knowledge is hard to interview for.

My largest project, Voices That Remain, is a digital archive with a 4-stage AI pipeline that uses GPT-5.4 with structured outputs, tool calling, and vision. It is 58,700 lines of TypeScript across 547 commits, deployed on GCP and live at voicesthatremain.com. The part that matters most for an FDE role is the human-in-the-loop design. I built review workflows into the pipeline because the people who validate AI outputs are not engineers. They need to understand what the system did, trust it or override it, and move on. Designing for that kind of user is the same skill an FDE uses when helping enterprise customers adopt a technical product.

When I built Staffclaw, I started by reverse-engineering 92 API endpoints from an existing scheduling platform in a single session. I mapped the data model, understood the business logic, and built a full stack replacement on top of it. That process of dropping into an unfamiliar system, figuring out how it works, and building something better is what FDEs do when they walk into a customer's environment.

I wrote 14,000 lines of Python by hand for SonicGen before I ever touched an AI coding agent. I did this on purpose through all of 2025. When I switched to agent-directed development in January 2026, I already had the fluency to catch mistakes and make architectural decisions without waiting for the agent to suggest them. For a role that requires helping enterprise teams adopt AI-powered development, having been through that transition myself is a real advantage.
