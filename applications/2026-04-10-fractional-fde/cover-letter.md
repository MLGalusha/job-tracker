# Cover Letter — Fractional AI, Forward-Deployed AI Engineer (Founding Team)

When I built Staffclaw, I started by reverse-engineering 92 API endpoints from an existing scheduling platform. I mapped the data model, figured out the business logic, and then built a full stack replacement. That process of dropping into an unfamiliar system, understanding how it works, and building something better on top of it is exactly what a forward-deployed engineer does every week.

My largest project, Voices That Remain, is a digital archive with a 4-stage AI pipeline that uses GPT-5.4 with structured outputs, tool calling, and vision. It is 58,700 lines of TypeScript across 547 commits, deployed on GCP, and live at voicesthatremain.com. I designed human-in-the-loop review workflows into the pipeline because the end users who validate AI outputs are not engineers. They need clear interfaces and confidence in what the system is telling them. That kind of thinking, building for the people who will actually use the system, is what separates a deployed solution from a demo.

I wrote 14,000 lines of Python by hand for SonicGen before I ever touched an AI coding agent. I did this on purpose through all of 2025. When I switched to agent-directed development in January 2026, I already had the fluency to catch mistakes, refactor confidently, and make architectural decisions without waiting for the agent to suggest them. For a forward-deployed role where you are shipping into client environments with real constraints and real deadlines, that foundation matters more than years on a resume.

One thing I find compelling about Fractional AI's model is that you are building custom solutions, not a single product. Every client engagement is a new problem with a new domain. The healthcare and life sciences focus means your FDEs need to care about data validation and auditability, not just shipping fast. I already build those patterns into my pipelines because I learned that LLM outputs need guardrails before they touch production.
