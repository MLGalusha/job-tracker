## What Bandwidth actually does

Bandwidth is a Communications Platform as a Service (CPaaS) company. They sell APIs that let enterprises embed voice calling, SMS/MMS messaging, and 911 emergency services into their software. The key differentiator: Bandwidth owns and operates its own voice network, unlike Twilio and competitors who resell carrier capacity. This means lower latency, better reliability, and higher margins on voice.

Customers include AWS, Microsoft, Google, Zoom, RingCentral, Genesys, Five9, Uber, and DocuSign. Revenue splits roughly 60% voice / 40% messaging. They serve 90% of the global economy across 65+ countries.

## The AI thesis

CEO David Morken's bet: "The next 1 billion users of the PSTN are AI voice agents." This is real, not marketing. AI voice calls generate 3-4x the revenue of standard calls because agents make more calls, longer calls, and need more sophisticated routing.

Key AI products and moves:
- **Maestro:** AI voice agent orchestration platform. 100% attach rate on enterprise deals. Lets enterprises deploy, manage, and route AI voice agents on Bandwidth's network.
- **MCP server:** Shipped an MCP server so AI agents can call Bandwidth APIs with natural language.
- **Activation Agent:** Automates number provisioning via agentic workflows.
- **Enterprise wins:** Won four large enterprise deals (top-10 US bank, major insurer) away from Verizon/AT&T/Lumen.

## The R&D team

The R&D team is separate from Bandwidth's main product engineering org. It is described as "small, agile, and T-shaped" with a startup mentality. The team's job is to explore emerging AI/ML technologies and build proof-of-concepts that could become Bandwidth products. They feed prototypes into the Maestro platform and other production teams.

This is prototype work: build fast, demo to leadership, evaluate whether it ships. The team uses Cursor and LLM-powered IDEs as daily tools. They experiment with RAG, agentic systems, advanced prompting, and multi-modal models.

## Grounded decisions (for cover letter and interview)

1. **Owns the network.** Bandwidth built its own voice network instead of reselling. This gives them margin and latency advantages that matter for real-time AI voice agents.
2. **Maestro as platform play.** Rather than building point solutions, they built an orchestration layer. This is the right abstraction for a market where AI agent frameworks change monthly.
3. **MCP server.** Shipping an MCP server shows the R&D team understands the agentic ecosystem and builds for interoperability.
4. **"Next 1B PSTN users are AI agents."** This is a bold, specific thesis. It orients the entire company around a real market shift, not vague "AI-powered" marketing.
