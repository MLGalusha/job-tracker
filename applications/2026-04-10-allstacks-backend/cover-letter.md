# Cover Letter — Allstacks, Backend Engineer

Hi Allstacks team,

I wrote a 14,000 line audio fingerprinting engine in Python by hand, without a single line from an AI agent. That was deliberate. I spent all of 2025 coding manually because I wanted to understand Python at depth before I started directing agents to write it for me. SonicGen does Shazam style audio matching with spectral peak detection, constellation hashing, and a restartable ingestion pipeline that can be killed and resumed cleanly. I built the whole thing solo.

What drew me to Allstacks is the size of the team and the reporting structure. Seventeen people, backend engineer reporting to the CTO. That is the kind of environment where every commit matters and there is no place to hide. My entire portfolio is self-directed work where I owned every decision from database schema to deployment pipeline, so high autonomy is where I do my best work. I have not used Django in production, but I know Python deeply, I know ORMs and Postgres from building with Drizzle, and I know REST API design from building Express backends with 92 reverse-engineered endpoints. Django's conventions are a framework switch, not a new paradigm.

I build primarily by directing coding agents, and they are my daily tools. I shipped Voices That Remain, a 58,700 line TypeScript production app with a multi stage AI pipeline, solo in about three weeks of focused work. I do not think I could have done that any other way. For a small team that needs to ship fast without hiring a large engineering org, agent directed velocity is a real multiplier. I review and own every decision, but the agents handle the raw typing.

The thing I find interesting about Allstacks as a product is that you are building analytics on top of engineering workflows, which means your backend has to ingest and normalize data from tools that all have different APIs, different schemas, and different assumptions about what a "project" or "task" even means. That normalization layer is a hard engineering problem, and it is the kind of problem I find genuinely interesting.

I am in Raleigh and can work from the downtown office or remote. I would like to learn more about the backend architecture.
