# Cover Letter -- Cognition, Product Engineer

I use AI coding agents as my primary development method. I have shipped 58,700 lines of production TypeScript through agent-directed development, and before that I wrote 14,000 lines of Python entirely by hand. I did both on purpose. The hand-coding year gave me the fluency to direct agents effectively, and the agent-directed work showed me where the workflow breaks down. For a company building the AI coding agent, that dual perspective is useful. I know what Devin's users need because I am one of them.

Voices That Remain is my largest project: a digital archive with a 4-stage AI pipeline that uses GPT-5.4 with structured outputs, tool calling, and vision. It is deployed on GCP and live at voicesthatremain.com. The product engineering decisions in that project are the ones I am most proud of. I designed human-in-the-loop review workflows because the end users are not engineers. I built guillemet tagging to mark uncertain text so reviewers focus where the model is least confident. I chose per-document-type prompts because letters, envelopes, telegrams, and photos need different processing. These are product decisions, not just engineering decisions, and they came from watching people actually use the system.

For Staffclaw, I saw a broken scheduling workflow and built a better one. I reverse-engineered 92 API endpoints from an existing platform, understood the domain logic, and built a full stack replacement that integrates external data sources like showtimes, weather, and historical shifts as input signals. The product insight was to build it as a transparent client: users log in with their existing credentials and every read/write proxies through the real API, so adoption requires zero migration. That kind of thinking about how people actually adopt tools is what I want to do at Cognition.

I am in Raleigh and ready to relocate to SF.
