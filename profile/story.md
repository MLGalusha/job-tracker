# Story

Mason's narrative — where he came from, how he got here, what he wants. Drafting skills (resume-tailor, cover-letter-writer) should read this for tone, momentum, and the threads worth pulling on.

## One-paragraph version (resume-ready)

Self-taught engineer who pivoted into AI-native software development after an early fascination with ChatGPT and generative tools in 2023. Completed UNC Chapel Hill's 6-month AI bootcamp in late 2024, where the capstone was training a real-time sheet-music generation model on GCP. Spent 2025 exploring coding agents — Cursor, then Claude Code and Codex as they matured late in the year. In early 2026 quit a full-time cooking job to go all-in on engineering, and shipped *Voices That Remain* (voicesthatremain.com), a production full-stack AI archive for historical personal letters, in roughly three weeks of focused work.

## Timeline

- **Late 2023:** Got interested in programming. Working through Harvard CS50. Already a heavy ChatGPT + Midjourney user from the day they launched — early adopter, not a late convert.
- **May/June 2024 → December 2024:** UNC Chapel Hill AI bootcamp (6 months). Capstone was a real-time sheet-music generation model — trained on GCP VMs with powerful GPUs, evaluated the model himself, did the majority of the work on what was nominally a group project.
- **November 2024:** Moved to Raleigh, NC. Took a full-time cooking job as a means to fund the bootcamp-to-tech transition.
- **2025:** Working 30–60 hrs/week as a cook while coding on the side. Dabbling with Cursor and early coding agents. Started *Truth Engine* — an ambitious dedup-and-context system for public-figure audio content (see `projects/truth-engine.md`). Paused it due to cost and scope.
- **January 2026:** Started *Voices That Remain* at the request of a family friend who had been collecting historical personal letters and wanted a public archive. Started coding solo, then pivoted within the first week to agent-driven development as Claude Code and Codex had matured enough to be the right primary tool.
- **March 2026:** Quit the cooking job to focus full-time on engineering. The job was paying bills but not compounding his skills, and the time sink was blocking real progress on VTR.
- **March–April 2026:** Shipped ~90% of VTR in roughly three weeks. Launched the site and a LinkedIn launch post. Currently iterating on VTR while starting a dedicated job search — which is what this job-tracker project is for.
- **Now (April 2026):** Full-time on engineering, broke, looking for his first real engineering role. Long-term aim after income stability: build a profitable software company of his own.

## Why AI-native, why agents

He's been a power AI user since 2023, before the market caught up. Coding *feels* like Minecraft to him with agents — one person can now build things that used to require a team, and he's intrinsically wired for that leverage. Agents aren't a tool he's adopting reluctantly; they're the thing that made the difference between "wanted to build software" and "actually shipped production software in three weeks." Any drafting skill should treat this as a *capability*, not a crutch — directing coding agents well is a real and increasingly valuable skill, and he's unusually good at it.
