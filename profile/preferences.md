# Preferences

What Mason is looking for and what to filter out. Used by `job-finder`, `company-researcher`, and `next-actions` skills when ranking roles and suggesting next steps.

## Target job lanes (ranked)

1. **Founding Engineer at pre-seed to Series A startups.** Primary target. Matches his build-from-scratch track record and agent-directed methodology.
2. **AI Engineer / Applied AI Engineer at AI-native companies.** Strong match — VTR demonstrates production AI pipeline work with structured outputs, tool calling, vision models, and HITL.
3. **Forward Deployed Engineer at AI labs (Anthropic, OpenAI).** The role is specifically well-suited to someone who's strong with agents and ships real products; Mason matches the hiring thesis.
4. **Full-Stack Engineer at AI-native startups** — Cursor, Replit, Vercel, Cognition, Sourcegraph, and similar. TypeScript-end-to-end experience is directly relevant.
5. **Local Raleigh / RTP roles.** Pryon is the best known local fit. Lower priority than the above but acceptable if agent-friendly.

## Skip lanes

- **Big tech (FAANG-adjacent).** Credential-filtered, not worth the effort without a degree or prior professional experience.
- **Enterprise / consultancies** (IBM, Cisco, Oracle, SAS, etc.). Not a fit for someone whose edge is agent-directed shipping speed.
- **Heavy LeetCode interview loops.** Time sink with low hit rate for his profile. If a company's process is 4+ rounds of algorithms whiteboarding, deprioritize.
- **Companies that actively restrict AI coding tools.** Not a hard block, but a soft red flag — Mason's core methodology is agent-directed, and a no-AI environment would nerf his primary edge.

- **Environments where coding agents are forbidden.** Not a hard no, but weight strongly against.
